Bricks are among the oldest, most durable building materials, with origins dating back to 7,000 BC. Brick is an exceptionally durable, sustainable building material with a lifespan often ranging from 100 to over 500 years. Made from clay, shale, or concrete, they are fired in kilns for strength, creating energy-efficient, fire-resistant, and sustainable structures. Common types include red clay (colored by iron oxide) and dense concrete.
